Overlays / Border boxes

Overlays are floating boxes that are suitable for superimposing on top of other elements. These elements are typically used for intermittent dialog boxes etc. The boxes default to 500 pixels wide, and auto height. Additional sizes may be added later. The overlays must be manually positioned in order to actually overlay other elements (or use glass panes, see below).

Modal dialogs/glass panes

If you want to create the illusion of a modal dialog, you can place your dialog boxes (typically border-box, but really anything) in a glass pane. The glass pane overlays a semi-transparent dark layer on the whole page, and centers whatever content you put in it. Thus, a glass pane with a border box will give you a "dimmed down" body with a border box hovering above it in the middle of the page. 

<div class="glass-pane">
  <div class="glass-pane-content">
    <div class="border-box overlay-m">
      <button ng-click="close()" class="action-btn border-box-action">
        <svg class="icon"><use xlink:href="#icon-x"/></svg>
      </button>
      <p class="mas">
        Some text in here
      </p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
